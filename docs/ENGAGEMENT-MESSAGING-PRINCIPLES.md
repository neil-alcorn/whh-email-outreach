## Goal

The first goal is awareness and relationship, not immediate fundraising.

A good outcome can be:

- a reply
- a social follow
- a social share
- a warm introduction
- an invitation to send more information
- a podcast or newsletter conversation
- permission to follow up later

## Core Outreach Frame

WHH's communication posture shifted from:

`Come see Haiti`

to:

`Let us bring Haiti to you`

Outreach should help aligned hosts, creators, churches, and business leaders encounter a hopeful, grounded Haiti story they may not see in the headlines.

## Best Pitch Posture

Avoid leading with:

`Can I be a guest?`

Prefer:

`We'd love to explore how we might collaborate.`

This feels less transactional and leaves room for better fits, including Haitian voices, social sharing, newsletter mentions, introductions, or future conversations.

## What To Personalize

Every first-touch email should include at least one specific note about:

- a recent episode, article, post, or ministry focus
- the audience they serve
- a shared value or mission theme
- why WHH's story may serve their people

Do not send a note that could obviously be sent unchanged to hundreds of shows.

## Strong Angles

- God is still working in Haiti.
- Homes, employment, and discipleship belong together.
- WHH is not just building houses; it is helping restore families.
- Employment is ministry and creates dignity.
- Haitian workers and leaders are partners, not beneficiaries.
- Stories from northern Haiti can offer hope without ignoring reality.

## Underused Angles

- worker profiles
- day-in-the-life content
- family follow-ups
- house anniversary stories
- pastor interviews
- Haitian leader interviews
- faith and work integration
- corporate sponsorship through employment, sustainability, and dignity

## Avoid

- generic guest requests
- pressure to donate
- fear-driven Haiti framing
- political blame
- construction details as the lead story
- treating Steve, Shelley, or Neil as the only possible voices

## Best-Fit Targets

The best targets are not always the largest. Prioritize contacts that are:

- mission-aligned
- audience-aligned
- relationship-friendly
- likely to care about faith, work, generosity, Haiti, missions, discipleship, poverty, or family transformation
