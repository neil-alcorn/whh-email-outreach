## Recommended Segments

1. Campaign donors since May 1, 2026
   - Purpose: thank and steward.
   - Tone: grateful, impact-oriented, no additional ask in the main body.
   - CTA: watch/share/follow YouTube and socials, optional survey.
   - Avoid: asking for another gift unless in a soft P.S.

2. LYBUNT donors
   - Purpose: warm reactivation.
   - Tone: "Your past support is still making an impact."
   - CTA: campaign give link, survey link, YouTube/social follow.
   - Avoid: saying "you have not given this year."

3. SYBUNT/prior donors
   - Purpose: gentle reconnection.
   - Tone: "You have been part of WHH's story before, and we wanted to share what God is still doing."
   - CTA: survey and social follow first, campaign give link second.
   - Avoid: heavy urgency or upgrade language.

4. Broad contacts/non-donors
   - Purpose: awareness and engagement.
   - Tone: "Despite everything you hear about Haiti, God is still working."
   - CTA: YouTube/social follow, campaign page, optional give.

5. Major and high-relationship donors
   - Purpose: relational stewardship.
   - Tone: should not receive the same broad mail merge if Steve, Shelley, Richard, or Neil should personally call/write.
   - CTA: personal contact, not mass survey-first.

## Message Guardrails

Safe personalization:

- First name.
- Segment-level language.
- General "past generosity" when donation history is verified.
- "Thank you for being part of this spring's campaign" when campaign gift is verified.
- "Because teams cannot regularly travel to Haiti right now, we are working harder to bring the stories to you" as a broad, honest context statement.

Avoid:

- Exact gift amount/date in mail merge.
- "We noticed you have not given."
- Internal labels like LYBUNT, SYBUNT, mid-level, major, lapsed.
- Overstated impact tied to a single person's gift.
- Legacy giving in this broad Spring Campaign follow-up.
- Anything that implies Neil personally wrote every individual note unless he actually reviews/sends that note.
- Making violence, gangs, or instability the emotional center of donor emails.
- Suggesting WHH paused or shrank because teams cannot visit. The stronger truth is that the connection is harder, but the mission has continued and accelerated by God's grace through Haitian leadership.

## Initial Copy Direction

Campaign donor thank-you:

- Lead with thanks.
- Share that the campaign is about homes, jobs, and discipleship.
- Invite them to watch/share/follow so more people can see what God is doing in Haiti.
- Ask for survey feedback after the thank-you, not before it.

LYBUNT reactivation:

- Lead with gratitude for past partnership, not donor inactivity.
- Tell a brief Peterson/home/employment story that shows homes, jobs, and discipleship together.
- Emphasize that a gift does more than help one family: it helps provide meaningful employment, strengthens the local economy, and supports Haitian workers serving Haitian families.
- Mention the match without pressure.
- Invite a gift, survey response, or social follow as three good next steps.

SYBUNT reconnection:

- Lead with reconnection and gratitude.
- Emphasize that WHH is still active despite Haiti headlines and despite the fact that U.S. teams have not been able to travel regularly since 2020.
- Use the "bring Haiti to you" frame: video, stories, social updates, and YouTube become relationship bridges while travel remains blocked.
- Put survey/social first and give link second.

## Recommendation

Use Richard's May 15 LYBNT/SYBNT draft as source material, not as the final send. The best next move is to build a segmented but modest outreach sequence: thank current campaign donors, warmly reactivate LYBUNT donors, gently reconnect SYBUNT/prior donors, and keep the planned/legacy giving question for a later targeted relationship track.
