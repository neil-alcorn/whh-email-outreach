## Durable Strategy

Richard's core framework is:

1. Listen.
2. Ask.
3. Thank.
4. Report.
5. Repeat.

Fundraising should be relationship-centered and data-informed. The goal is not a single transaction; it is lifelong partnership.

Strategic priorities:

- Improve donor retention.
- Convert first-time donors into second-gift donors.
- Cultivate mid-level donors as the major donor pipeline.
- Steward major donors personally.
- Use structured listening through surveys and donor conversations.
- Treat planned/legacy giving as a board-level strategic decision and later targeted follow-up, not a broad Spring Campaign add-on.
- Integrate direct mail, email, social media, phone calls, and survey work so each channel reinforces the same campaign story.

## WHH Model Update

Teams have not been able to travel to Haiti regularly since 2020. COVID, the July 2021 assassination of President Jovenel Moise, ongoing instability, travel restrictions, and insurance barriers have made in-person connection much harder.

This should not make the donor message smaller or more fearful. It should clarify the need:

- The relationship gap is wider because supporters cannot easily go see the work.
- The need is greater, but hope is still growing.
- By God's grace, the mission has continued and accelerated through Haitian leadership.
- WHH is not only helping a family move into a home; WHH is creating meaningful employment, strengthening the local economy, and supporting discipleship.
- Social media, video, YouTube, email, surveys, and storytelling are now relationship bridges: "Let us bring Haiti to you."

## Messaging Principles From Richard

Use:

- Donor-as-partner language.
- Short paragraphs and scan-friendly structure.
- Clear, warm, invitational asks.
- Stories first, data second.
- Actual costs/outcomes when using gift arrays.
- Pictures and video links that relate directly to the story and call to action.
- Steve/Shelley voice checks before campaign sends.
- Faith language that is direct enough for WHH stakeholders: God, Jesus, church, discipleship, prayer.

Avoid:

- Drastic shifts from what donors are used to hearing from WHH.
- Treating donors as people helping WHH as an institution rather than partners in the work itself.
- Long dense paragraphs.
- Unsupported numbers.
- Fear-driven Haiti framing as the center of the message.
- Sending drafts externally before all key internal voices sign off.

Richard explicitly noted: anything circulated internally should be treated as draft until signed off.

## Donor Contact Scripts

Richard provided scripts for major, mid-level, and LYBNT/SYBNT donor calls.

Principles:

- Scripts are guides, not copy to read verbatim.
- Start by thanking and listening.
- Ask whether contact info is current.
- In true one-to-one calls, it is acceptable to reference first gift date, recent gift date, lifetime giving, and milestones if the data is accurate and the relationship context supports it.
- In mass or semi-mass email, do not reference detailed giving history.
- Ask clearly but warmly.
- If the answer is no, thank them sincerely and close graciously.
- If appropriate, ask how to pray for them.

Contact outcomes to record:

- Date of contact.
- Email or phone channel.
- Interest or concerns.
- Contact info updates.
- Whether they intend to give and, if volunteered, amount.
- Whether they are open to further contact.
- Prayer requests or personal notes that are appropriate to retain.

## Salesforce And Reporting Guidance

Richard wants reports and dashboards that help leadership understand both activity and results.

Dashboard/reporting concepts:

- Distinguish retained, renewed, and new donors so totals reconcile.
- Compare YTD giving and donor counts to the same period in previous years.
- Prefer prior month/quarter comparisons over "this month" if data is not real time.
- Include Total Donors YTD, Total Given YTD, Avg Gift, Median Gift, Monthly Donors, Retained Donors, Renewed Donors, and New Donors.
- Include five-year comparison tables for total donors/giving and major/mid/core donor counts and dollars.
- Review major/mid-level thresholds; Richard suggested deriving current-year thresholds from recent-year segment cutoffs rather than fixed annual rules.
- Lead indicators should track controllable engagement: emails sent, phone calls, meetings, handwritten notes, solicitations.
- Lead indicators should be segmented: major, mid-level, LYBNT/SYBNT, first-time donors.
- Add or surface primary relationship owner for donors.

Salesforce/contact tracking:

- Richard wants access to all major, mid-level, LYBNT/SYBNT donor accounts, not just current-year donors.
- Salesforce should be used to record campaign calls, contact outcomes, follow-ups, and donor relationship notes.
